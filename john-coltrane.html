John Coltrane

He could get in his space ship and it would take him anywhere he wanted to go

Grammy-winning jazz saxophonist/composer John Coltrane enthralled audiences with his experimental, avant-garde jazz.

Here is a time line of John Coltrane's life:

  • 1926 - Born in Hamlet, North Carolina.
  • 1926 - The family relocated to High Point, North Carolina where he attended high school.
  • 1939 - Tragedy strikes- shortly after graduating Grammar school, Coltrane's father, uncle and grandfather passed away leaving his mother to take care of the family.
  • 1939 - 13-year old John Coltrane first picked up the alto saxophone.
  • 1949 - First recording with Dizzy Gillepsie's band. Coltrane stayed in numerous bands with Dizzy until being kicked out for alcohol and heroin abuse.
  • 1955 - Married Naima (née Juanita Grubbs), a Muslim convert, who heavily influenced his spirituality. When they married, she had a five-year-old daughter named Antonia, later named Saeeda. Coltrane adopted Saeeda. The love ballad he wrote to honor his wife, "Naima," was Coltrane's favorite composition.
  • 1955 - Coltrane recorded his first session with the Davis quintet.
  • 1956 - Performed a tenor madness session with Sonny Rollins, another phenomenal jazz artist.
  • 1957 - First recording session with famous jazz trombonist Thelonius Monk.
  • 1957 - Coltrane becomes the leader of his own jazz group for the first time.
  • 1957 - Recorded his legendary "Blue Train (Trane)" album, taking jazz music on a completely different path.
  • 1958 - Records "Kind of Blue" with Miles Davis.
  • 1959 - Records the revolutionary album "Giant Steps".
  • 1960 - The Coltrane Quartet is formed. The original quartet members were: Steve Kuhn on piano, Steve Davis on bass, and Pete LaRoca on drums.
  • 1960 - Records "My Favorite Things".
  • 1961 - Records "Africa Brass".
  • 1962 - The Classic Quartet recorded "Coltrane", the first recording featuring all new members of Coltrane's quartet.
  • 1963 - Parted ways with Naima Coltrane but they remained legally married until 1966.
  • 1963 - He met pianist Alice McLeod. They moved in together and had two sons before he was "officially divorced from Naima in 1966, at which time John and Alice were immediately married.
  • 1964 - Records "A Love Supreme".
  • 1967 - Performs at the Olatunji Concert, which was the last time he was recorded live.
  • 1967 - Dies at the age of 41 in Long Island, New York.
  • 1988 - Received a Grammy post-humously for his famous version of "My Favorite Things".
  • 2014 - Admitted into the Grammy Hall of Fame. He won nine Grammys throughout his career.

That’s what I would like to do. I think that’s one of the greatest things you can do in life and we all try to do it in some way. The musician’s is through his music."
